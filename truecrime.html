Fun Facts About True Crime

  • The first known serial killer was Gilles de Rais, a 15th-century French nobleman accused of murdering over 100 children.
  • The term “serial killer” was coined by the FBI in the 1970s to describe individuals who commit multiple murders over a period of time.
  • True crime shows often use reenactments to visualize events, but these reenactments can be dramatized for entertainment purposes.
  • The world’s most notorious criminal, Charles Manson, never actually committed any murders himself but manipulated his followers into doing so.
  • The “Black Dahlia” case remains one of the most famous unsolved murders in American history, with Elizabeth Short’s death still shrouded in mystery.
  • The 1996 movie “The Usual Suspects” popularized the term “The Kaiser Soze” which is a fictional criminal mastermind.
